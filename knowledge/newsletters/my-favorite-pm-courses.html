Q: What are the best PM courses out there?

We’re living in a golden age of courses. Courses as far as the eye can see. A great course can change your life (I know many people who’ve had transformational experiences), and a bad course can be a huge time and money suck. So you need to choose wisely. With the end of the year coming and L&D budgets expiring, it’s a great time to lock down a course.

Though I haven’t taken many PM courses recently, I did teach a course last year (that I’ve since put on pause to make time for other projects, like the podcast), and through my research prepping for the course, and this great Twitter thread, I feel like I’ve got a pretty good idea of which courses are worth your time. Below, I’m going to go out on a limb and recommend 10 PM courses to check out—five for senior PMs and five for up-and-coming PMs—plus a bunch of honorable mentions if you’re an overachiever.

I’m mostly limiting my recommendations to courses that are (1) live, (2) cohort-based, (3) have run a couple of cohorts, and (4) plan to run many more. This list is my best guess of what’s great. I’m sure I missed some and am unaware of others. Please let me know in the comments if you’ve taken a course that you’ve loved that I didn’t list.

5 courses to check out for senior PMs

  1. Managing your PM career in 2023 & beyond by Shreyas Doshi—just launched and is already beloved by one and all
  2. Product Strategy by Reforge—a foundational Reforge course, created by the greats Casey Winters and Fareed Mosavat
  3. Product Strategy Workshop by Gibson Biddle—you need to see Gibson in action, especially now that he’s now teaching cohort-based courses on Maven
  4. Continuous Interviewing by Teresa Torres—will change the way you build product
  5. Product Leadership by Reforge—for budding managers, created by Ravi Mehta

5 PM courses to check out for up-and-coming PMs

  1. Product Management Foundations by Reforge—probably the best course out there for new PMs

  2. Simulator for Learning Data-Driven Product Management by Sean Ellis and Oleg Ya on GoPractice—I wasn’t aware of this one until recently. It’s not live or cohort-based, but I’m including it anyway because so many people have recommended it.

  3. Assumption Testing by Teresa Torres—will help you avoid wasting time on the wrong ideas

  4. The Growth Series by Reforge—never too early to start learning about growth
  5. The Product Principles Sprint by Section4 and Prof. Adam Alter—a recent discovery

Honorable mentions

Here are some additional courses to check out, including a few that are self-paced, in-person, or brand-new:

  1. Reforge courses

  2. Mastering Product Management

  3. Experimentation + Testing
  4. Retention + Engagement

  5. Maven courses

  6. Break into AI Product Management & Get Certified by Marily Nika

  7. Launch Your Product Management Career by Peter Yang
  8. Building B2B Marketing by Emily Kramer
  9. Product Psychology Bootcamp by Nir Eyal
  10. Influence Without Authority by Satish Mummareddy
  11. INSPIRED: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love by Silicon Valley Product Group (in-person workshop)
  12. Design Thinking Bootcamp (in person) or Product Management certificate program (online) by Stanford
  13. Becoming a Product Manager: A Complete Guide by Cole Mercer and Evan Kimbrell (self-paced and online)
  14. Insurjo and Upraised for folks based in India

Has any other course transformed how you build product? Are you working on a course that you’d love for me to check out? Let me know by leaving a comment 👇
